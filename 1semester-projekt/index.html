Why are sharks important?

In the pursuit of UN Goal 14: Life Below Water, the conservation of sharks emerges as a vital component. Sharks, as apex predators, play a crucial role in maintaining the delicate balance of marine ecosystems. Their presence helps regulate the populations of marine species, contributing to the overall health and diversity of our oceans.

While shark incidents, including rare shark attacks on humans, capture headlines, it's essential to approach these events with a broader perspective. Understanding the root causes of such incidents allows us to implement responsible conservation measures. By fostering coexistence between humans and sharks, we can work towards a sustainable future for both communities.

Sharks, often misunderstood and misrepresented, are key indicators of marine ecosystem health. The conservation efforts aligned with UN Goal 14 aim to protect these majestic creatures and acknowledge their significance in preserving the rich biodiversity of our oceans. As we strive for a balanced and harmonious relationship with the marine world, responsible shark conservation becomes a shared responsibility.

Gender and their correlation with fatality

When it comes to shark attacks, 3543 men have been attacked without fatal outcomes, where 1293 men have experienced attacks that were fatal.

On the women's side, 475 have been attacked without fatal outcomes, while 124 women have experienced fatal attacks.
